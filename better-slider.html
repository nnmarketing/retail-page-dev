Why Omega-3s

Essential fats at the foundation of who we are.

Essential Fats

Every body needs omega-3s

Your body is made of trillions of cells. Each one contains important fats, especially omega-3 fats, which your body can’t produce on its own.

Since about the mid-20th century, changes in food manufacturing have introduced soaring amounts of omega-6 fats into the American diet from things like soy, corn, and cottonseed oils, grain-fed meats, and most processed foods. Without trying, most people eat about 15–20 times more omega-6 than omega-3, even though a relative balancebetween the two is best.

Modest amounts of omega-6 fats are fine. But because omega-6s promote protective immune functions that are counter to those of omega-3s, and because both kinds of fats compete for limited space in cell membranes, our bodies end up in a heightened state of immune response without the means to easily resolve it. Too much omega-6. Not enough omega-3.

OMEGA-3s: how much is enough?

One of the most obvious questions from anyone interested in omega-3s is, how much should I take? It’s also one of the toughest, because there’s no definitive answer. A recommended daily amount for omega-3s has not been established the way it has for nutrients like vitamin C, or calcium, so health experts suggest daily intakes based on a person’s stage of life, health status, nutritional profile, and what the research shows. One thing is certain, however, omega-3s are essential for health across the lifespan.

Infants, Children, & Adolescents
Omega-3 recommendations for infants and children vary by age and weight, but the vast majority of children do not get enough omega-3s because they do not eat enough cold-water fish. Nursing also affects babies’ omega-3 status, as nursing delivers much of mom’s omega-3 stores to her child. In early childhood, DHA is particularly important because the brain relies heavily on DHA for growth and development. From about age four through adolescence, most children benefit from a higher ratio of EPA to DHA for general health.

Adults & Seniors
The combined advice of health experts around the world leads to a basic rule of thumb: Adults need a minimum of 500 mg EPA+DHA daily for optimal health. However, more than this amount (sometimes much more) is recommended for specific health concerns. The American Heart Association (AHA) recommends that individuals with documented Coronary Heart Disease consume 1000 mg EPA+DHA. For people with high triglycerides they recommend 2000–4000 mg.1 No adjustments to intake are indicated for seniors. Finally, it is worth noting that the U.S. FDA set a ‘Generally Regarded as Safe’level of 3000 mg per day for omega-3s.

During Pregnancy
EPA and DHA are the two most beneficial omega-3 fats. Your doctor may or may not mention the importance of prenatal omega-3 DHA, so it’s good to know what professional organizations recommend. The American Pregnancy Association (APA) recognizes the need to get adequate DHA. The APA advises a minimum of 300 mg DHA daily during pregnancy.2 This figure is based on the recommendations of the International Society for the Study of Fatty Acids and Lipids (ISSFAL).

Manufacturing Process

Most concentrated fish oils on the market today are formulated for cost-saving. The manufacturing process is cut short, and they end up with omega-3s as ethyl ester molecules, synthetic fats with only about 30 years of history in the human diet.

At Nordic Naturals, all our omega-3 fish oil products are produced in the triglyceride form, the same form that’s naturally found in fish, and the form your body most easily recognizes and absorbs. Triglyceride-form fish oils require more manufacturing steps, and take longer to produce, but for us, there’s no other way.

Fish Oils

Source Matters

Omega-3 fatty acids come from two main sources: plants (like flax), and cold-water and algae. People mistakenly think these two sources are equally beneficial. While both are helpful, marine sources of omega-3s are far superior for long-term health.*

Flax contains alpha-linolenic acid (ALA), which must be converted to the longer-chain omega-3s fats EPA and DHA. These are the primary fats that have been shown to benefit health. Importantly, research also shows that even under optimal lifestyle and health conditions, only about 5% of ALA converts to EPA, and just 1% converts to DHA. In simple terms, a 10-ounce grocery bag of flaxmeal has the equivalent EPA+DHA as a single serving of Nordic Naturals fish oil!

Fish oil is the most reliable source of the omega-3s we need most. Unfortunately, few people take fish oil regularly. No wonder 90% of us are deficient in these essential nutrients.

Raw Material

Nordic Naturals starts with the best raw material available. Our meticulous quality control begins in the ocean with our careful choice of fish species that are naturally low in toxins and high in omega-3 fats.

All our omega-3 products are made exclusively from three types of wild fish. None of the fish we use is endangered, and all are caught from some of the cleanest waters in the world. We respect important fishing regulations when sourcing our raw materials, including quotas, minimum catch sizes, fishing ground closures, and fishing methods.

Manufactoring without chemicals or excessive heat

We use several proprietary and patented steps in processing our oils, from water and clay filters, to molecular distillation with no chemicals or excessive heat, and all while preserving the natural constituents of the oil to the highest degree possible. Our purification process removes environmental toxins (heavy metals, dioxins, PCBs, etc.) and saturated fats, leaving only the beneficial components of the fish oil. We utilize a proprietary (oxygen-free) nitrogen environment to achieve our leading freshness levels, and to further reduce heat requirements, thereby maintaining the integrity of the oils.
